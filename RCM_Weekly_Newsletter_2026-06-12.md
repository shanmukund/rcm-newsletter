## Section 01 — Regulatory / Payment Updates

### URGENT — June 15 Deadline: Comment on CMS Drug Prior Authorization Interoperability Rule Before the Window Closes

CMS's 2026 Interoperability Standards and Prior Authorization for Drugs proposed rule (CMS-0062-P) has a public comment deadline of **June 15, 2026** — three days from publication of this issue. The rule extends the 2024 electronic prior authorization framework beyond nondrug items and services to drugs covered under both medical and pharmacy benefits, introducing shorter decision timelines, electronic PA requirements, standardized data exchange, and more specific denial explanations. If finalized, most requirements would take effect October 1, 2027.

**Key proposed requirements:**
- All HIPAA-covered entities must support electronic prior authorization for drugs
- Decision timelines shortened to align CMS programs (urgent requests within 24 hours; standard within 72 hours for MA/Medicaid)
- Payers must provide specific denial explanations — not just generic "medical necessity not established"
- Standardized FHIR-based data exchange for drug PA transactions

**Stat:** Comments on CMS-0062-P are due **June 15, 2026**. If finalized, the rule will affect every practice billing specialty drugs under Medicare, Medicaid, and Medicaid Managed Care.

The CY 2026 Medicare Physician Fee Schedule remains in effect. The 3.26% conversion factor increase and the permanent telehealth extensions from CMS-1832-F are now fully operational. However, the 2.5% efficiency adjustment applied to approximately 7,700 procedural and diagnostic wRVU values continues to compress reimbursement for procedural specialties — cardiology, orthopedics, radiology, and surgery face the sharpest impacts.

## Section 02 — Prior Authorization / Payer Relations

### Massachusetts PA Reform Now Live: Chronic Disease, Cancer Scans & Emergency Care No Longer Require Preapproval

Massachusetts Governor Healey's final regulations eliminating prior authorization for routine and essential health care took effect June 5, 2026 — making Massachusetts the most aggressive state PA reformer in the nation. Practices billing Massachusetts commercial payers can now submit claims for these services without preapproval:

- **Emergency and urgent care** — PA requirement eliminated
- **Primary care visits** — PA requirement eliminated
- **Cancer scans** — PA eliminated
- **Chronic condition medications** — including asthma inhalers, diabetes drugs, and heart disease therapies
- **Inpatient acute care** — PA eliminated
- **Post-acute services on weekends and holidays** — PA eliminated
- **Substance use disorder treatment** — PA eliminated
- **Physical and occupational therapy** — PA eliminated

**Key payer obligations under the new rules:** Insurers must respond within **24 hours** for urgently needed care, provide specific denial explanations, and honor continuity of care protections.

Virginia's new PA law — establishing minimum decision durations and AI oversight requirements for PA systems — also took effect in June 2026, adding oversight obligations for payers using algorithmic PA decision tools.

Nationally, AHIP and the Blue Cross Blue Shield Association jointly reported an **11% reduction in prior authorizations** since the 2025 voluntary industry commitments. CMS's proposed drug PA rule (comment deadline June 15) would mandate electronic PA for drugs with FHIR-based data exchange.

**Stat:** 5 states have enacted significant PA reform legislation effective in 2026. Practices billing Massachusetts commercial patients must update intake workflows immediately — submitting unnecessary PA requests for eliminated categories creates administrative burden with no reimbursement benefit.

## Section 04 — Coding Updates

### Radiation Oncology Billing Overhaul: New Level Codes Replace Simple/Intermediate/Complex; IMRT Codes 77385–77386 Deleted

The CPT 2026 code set — with 288 new codes, 84 deletions, and 46 revisions — contains the most significant overhaul to radiation oncology billing in more than a decade. Practices providing radiation therapy must audit and update charge masters immediately.

**Radiation Therapy Delivery (Effective January 1, 2026):**
- **Old codes 77402, 77407, 77412** (simple/intermediate/complex radiation treatment delivery) are replaced by **Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3** codes with revised clinical descriptors
- **IMRT codes 77385 and 77386** are **deleted** — practices still billing these codes will receive CO-4 denials

**Cardiology Coding Impacts:**
- **CPT 33340** (Percutaneous left atrial appendage occlusion/LAAO): wRVU cut **26.8%** — from 14.00 to 10.25 wRVUs. This is the most impactful reimbursement reduction for interventional cardiology in 2026
- **Percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) code family:** Entire family revised with clarified definitions for major coronary arteries, branches, segments, and lesions — a single lesion spanning multiple segments does not qualify as multiple reportable lesions

**Key coding note for all specialties:** The CPT 2026 set also added 288 new codes for digital health, AI-augmented services, and care coordination. Four new Principal Illness Navigation (PIN) codes (G0023, G0024, G0140, G0146) reimburse individualized chronic care navigation — high-value codes for primary care and chronic disease management practices.

**Stat:** 418 total CPT changes in 2026 — 288 new codes, 84 deletions, 46 revisions. Every practice should have completed charge master review by now; if not, run a prospective denial audit against the 2026 code set before the next billing cycle.

## Section 05 — Revenue Velocity / KPIs

### Revenue Velocity Benchmarks: Days in AR 30–40, Clean Claims ≥95%, and the Aging Ratios That Predict Cash Flow

The industry has converged on a set of measurable benchmarks for 2026 that separate high-performing practices from those silently leaking revenue. Here is the definitive 2026 KPI benchmark set for medical practice RCM:

**Days in Accounts Receivable (AR):**
- Industry benchmark: **30–40 days**
- Top performers: **under 25 days**
- Specialty median range: 33–42 days depending on payer mix and specialty complexity
- Alert threshold: any practice above 50 days in AR needs immediate intervention

**Clean Claims Rate:**
- Industry floor: **≥95%**
- Best-in-class: **98–99%+**
- Revenue impact: every 1% below clean claims benchmark costs approximately **0.3–0.5 days in AR** — because rework adds 30–60 days to a touched claim's collection cycle

**Payment Velocity by Payer Class (clean submission to payment):**
- Medicare FFS: **14 days** (14-day prompt-pay rule)
- Commercial (UHC, Aetna, Cigna, BCBS): **15–22 days**
- Medicaid (varies by state): 21–45 days

**AR Aging Distribution (healthy practice):**
- 0–30 days: **65–75%** of total AR
- 31–60 days: **12–18%**
- 61–90 days: **6–10%**
- 91+ days: **under 12–15%** combined

**Revenue velocity optimization levers for 2026:**
- Real-time denial dashboards: teams acting in days vs. weeks report faster denial overturn rates and shorter AR cycles
- AI-powered predictive denial scoring before claim submission is now moving from pilot to production at best-in-class organizations
- Automation of high-volume eligibility checks at every patient encounter (not just first visit) eliminates the #1 source of preventable front-end denials

**Stat:** The American Medical Association estimates up to **20% of medical claims** are denied on first submission — and most of those denials are preventable at the point of capture. With AI-native RCM, denial rates drop to ~5.7%; manual shops run 12–22%.

## Section 07 — Compliance Corner

### Compliance Deadlines Pile Up: Drug PA Comment Period Closes June 15, Medicaid Community Engagement State Plans Due, Price Transparency Percentile Reporting Active

Three compliance obligations converge this week — one with a hard three-day deadline.

**URGENT — CMS Drug PA Rule Comment Deadline: June 15, 2026**
The proposed rule (CMS-0062-P) extending electronic PA requirements to drugs closes for public comment on June 15. Specialty practices that prescribe high-cost drugs, biologics, and chemotherapy agents have the most at stake. Comments can be submitted at regulations.gov referencing CMS-0062-P. Key concerns from specialty practices include whether the proposed 72-hour standard decision timeline is operationally realistic for complex oncology PA requests.

**Medicaid Community Engagement Implementation (States Deadline: January 1, 2027)**
The CMS interim final rule (CMS-2454-IFC, effective June 1, 2026) requires states to implement 80-hour/month work requirements for non-elderly, non-pregnant Medicaid adults by January 1, 2027. CMS estimates **2.3 million individuals will lose Medicaid coverage in 2027** and roughly 3.2 million annually thereafter. Practices with significant Medicaid patient panels must:
- Model potential impact on patient volumes and payer mix by specialty
- Prepare financial counseling workflows for patients transitioning to self-pay or marketplace coverage
- Review financial assistance and charity care policies

**Hospital Price Transparency — Percentile Data Now Required**
Effective January 1, 2026, hospitals must calculate and encode the **10th percentile, median, and 90th percentile** allowed amounts for each item or service — along with the total number of remittances used to calculate them. CMS enforcement of these expanded requirements began April 1, 2026. Hospitals not in compliance face civil monetary penalties up to $2 million per year for large facilities.

**Stat:** CMS estimates Medicaid community engagement rules will result in 3.2 million annual coverage losses after 2028 — and the $580/month earnings threshold (80 × federal minimum wage) creates a precise targeting floor for patient financial screening conversations.

## Section 09 — Specialty RCM Spotlight

### Specialty RCM Spotlight: Primary Care, Cardiology, Orthopedics, Oncology, Neurology, Mental Health

**Primary Care:** Massachusetts PA reform directly benefits primary care practices — eliminating PA for primary care visits, chronic condition medications (diabetes, asthma, heart disease), and post-acute weekend/holiday services. The 4 new Principal Illness Navigation codes (G0023, G0024, G0140, G0146) create new billable encounters for individualized chronic care navigation — practices should verify these codes are active in their charge master.

**Cardiology:** Two simultaneous hits this week. (1) CPT 33340 (LAAO/Watchman) wRVU declined 26.8% from 14.00 to 10.25 — the most significant cardiology reimbursement cut in 2026. (2) The entire PCI family was revised; a single lesion spanning multiple segments no longer qualifies as multiple reportable lesions. Cardiologists billing PCI without updating to the revised descriptors risk downcoding and CO-4 denials. Also: echocardiogram PA eliminated under the CY 2027 MA rule for 50 million UHC members (effective since last week).

**Orthopedics:** CMS's strict 2026 interpretation of global surgical packages narrows what is separately billable during pre- and post-operative periods. Combined with the 2.5% wRVU efficiency adjustment hitting surgical codes, orthopedic practices should audit global period documentation and confirm modifier-57 use is defensible. Annual revenue exposure from global package misinterpretation can exceed $40,000 per surgeon in a 3-physician orthopedic group.

**Oncology:** Radiation therapy billing undergoes its biggest change since 2013. The simple/intermediate/complex delivery codes (77402, 77407, 77412) are replaced by Level 1/2/3 codes. IMRT codes 77385 and 77386 are **deleted** — any outstanding claims or templates using these codes must be updated. Four new PIN codes support oncology patient navigation reimbursement. Principal Illness Navigation codes G0023 and G0140 are specifically applicable to oncology care coordination.

**Neurology:** NCCI modifier audit remains a priority. Incorrect modifier 26/TC usage is the leading source of CO-4 denials in neurology. Neurophysiologic monitoring, EEG, and EMG billing against the 2026 NCCI tables should be verified before each billing cycle.

**Mental Health:** Virginia's new PA AI oversight law — requiring transparency and human review override capabilities for algorithmic PA decisions — is effective in June 2026. Practices in Virginia billing behavioral health services to commercial payers should verify payer compliance with the new law. Nationally, telehealth behavioral health coverage extensions under the CY 2026 PFS are now permanent — a meaningful billing stability win for independent mental health practices.
