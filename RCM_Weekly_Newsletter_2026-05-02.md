## Section 01 — Regulatory/Payment Updates
### H.R. 1 Signed Into Law: Medicaid Cuts, Provider Tax Freeze, and a One-Year Medicare Lifeline

H.R. 1, the sweeping federal reconciliation bill, was signed into law this week, triggering the most significant Medicaid financing restructuring in decades. For physician practices, the consequences fall into three immediate buckets.

**Provider Tax Freeze:** H.R. 1 freezes existing state provider tax programs and bars any new taxes. Medicaid expansion states must phase down the maximum allowable provider tax rate from 6% to 3.5% by 2032. This directly constrains states' ability to use provider taxes to draw down federal matching funds — a critical revenue mechanism for many safety-net practices.

**State-Directed Payment Caps:** The bill caps state-directed payments at either 100% or 110% of Medicare rates depending on a state's expansion status. Practices in high-cost markets that benefit from supplemental Medicaid payments will see those payments compressed.

**Eligibility Redeterminations:** Work requirements and more frequent eligibility checks (every 6 months) will create coverage gaps, increasing uncompensated care and bad debt for practices with Medicaid-heavy panels.

The one piece of near-term relief: H.R. 1 provides a **2.5% Medicare physician payment increase beginning January 1, 2026** — temporary relief that does not address the underlying SGR-driven structural shortfall.

**Key Stat:** Practices in Medicaid expansion states stand to lose an estimated $0.30–$0.60 per Medicaid dollar billed as state-directed payments compress toward Medicare floor rates.

## Section 02 — Prior Authorization / Payer Relations
### PA Reform Scoreboard: Aetna at 88%, Cigna Targeting 70% — But Denials Keep Rising

The spring PA reform progress report is in, and the picture is mixed. Major payers are hitting standardization milestones while overall denial volumes continue to climb.

- **Aetna** has standardized 88% of its prior authorization volume and claims the fewest medical services requiring PA among national health plans.
- **Cigna** expects to standardize more than 70% of its PA volume for medical services by end of 2026, adding services on a rolling basis.
- **UnitedHealthcare and Aetna** backed new PA standards alongside Elevance Health in late April, committing to further real-time response capabilities by 2027.

Meanwhile, UHC is offering plans in **109 fewer counties** and Aetna in **100 fewer counties** for 2026 — meaning some patients will need to find new in-network providers, creating eligibility and PA verification disruption at the front end.

The CMS Drug PA Proposed Rule (CMS-0062-P) — which extends FHIR-based electronic PA to drug benefits — has a **comment deadline of June 15, 2026**. Practices that prescribe high-cost drugs under Part B should weigh in.

**Key Stat:** Health plans serving 270 million Americans are participating in the AHIP/BCBSA multi-year PA reform initiative — yet appeal overturn rates remain above 80% at major MA plans, signaling that denials are still predominantly inappropriate.

## Section 04 — Coding Updates
### CPT 2026: 288 New Codes Including First-Ever AI-Augmented Billing; ICD-10 Diabetes Remission Guidance Now Active

The 2026 CPT code set — 418 total changes — is the first in history to explicitly recognize AI in medical coding. Key updates affecting physician practices:

**AI-Augmented Services:** The AMA introduced codes for AI-assisted diagnostic interpretation, marking the first time AI augmentation is a billable differentiator in the CPT system.

**Cardiology:** New PCI code **92930** covers stent placement involving two or more distinct coronary lesions or bifurcation lesions. Lower extremity revascularization (LER) codes are restructured. Coronary plaque analysis has new dedicated codes.

**Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM):** Tightened documentation requirements demand precise tracking of time spent reviewing patient data, communicating with patients about readings, and adjusting treatment plans. Sloppy RPM billing is now high audit risk.

**ICD-10-CM Guideline Updates (effective FY2026):**
- **Diabetes Remission:** Assign **E11.A** (Type 2 DM without complications, in remission) when provider documentation confirms remission — previously undercoded.
- **HIV:** Assign **Z21** for HIV-positive patients on antiretroviral therapy with no active HIV disease, illness, or AIDS documentation — shifting from older B20 sequencing practices.

**Key Stat:** 487 new ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes were added for FY2026, with 38 revisions and 28 deletions — the largest single-year code volume increase since 2016.

## Section 07 — Compliance Corner
### H.R. 1 Creates New Admin Burden; CMS Drug PA Comment Deadline June 15

Three compliance priorities demand attention this week:

**H.R. 1 Administrative Impact:** Work requirement verification, biannual eligibility redeterminations, and enhanced data matching will require practices to update registration workflows. Expect increased front-desk burden verifying Medicaid eligibility at every encounter — not just annually.

**Price Transparency Enforcement (Active Since April 1):** CMS enforcement of updated MRF requirements is now live. Hospitals and ambulatory surgical centers must publish median, 10th percentile, and 90th percentile allowed amounts (not just estimated amounts). Practices billing in hospital outpatient settings need to confirm their facility partners are compliant — payer audits of chargemaster data are expected to increase.

**CMS-0062-P Drug PA Proposed Rule — Comments Due June 15:** The rule would extend FHIR-based electronic PA to drug benefits beginning October 1, 2027. Practices administering Part B drugs (oncology infusions, biologics, etc.) should submit comments. Key provisions: real-time PA decisions for drugs, specific denial explanations, standardized data exchange.

**Key Stat:** Hospitals that voluntarily waive their right to a hearing on price transparency violations can reduce their penalty by **35%** — a new compliance negotiation lever that went live January 1, 2026.

## Section 08 — Independent Practice Watch
### H.R. 1 Is an Existential Test for Medicaid-Dependent Practices

Physician ownership of practices fell to **42.2%** in 2026, and H.R. 1 will accelerate consolidation pressure on the practices that remain independent. The financial math is getting harder:

- Provider tax revenues funding Medicaid supplemental payments are being frozen then phased down
- State-directed payments capped at Medicare rates remove above-Medicare Medicaid payment floors
- Every 6-month eligibility redetermination creates coverage gap risk at each encounter
- Work requirement administration adds front-desk and billing overhead with no reimbursement

**Where You Should Be — Independent Practice Benchmarks:**

| Metric | Where You Should Be | Industry Average | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Days in A/R | <25 days | 40–50 days | Critical |
