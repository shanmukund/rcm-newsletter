## Section 01 — Regulatory / Payment Updates

### Medicare Advantage Network Crisis Deepens: 21 Health Systems Exit, 2.9 Million Enrollees Forced to Switch — Revenue Risk Models Every Practice Needs Now

The Medicare Advantage network instability that began with isolated contract disputes has escalated into the most significant structural disruption to practice revenue in over a decade. In 2026, at least **21 major health systems** have terminated or declined to renew Medicare Advantage contracts — forcing approximately **2.9 million MA enrollees** (roughly 1 in 10) to switch plans mid-coverage period.

**Who has exited or is exiting MA networks in 2026:**
- **Mayo Clinic**: Out of network for UnitedHealthcare individual MA plans and all Humana MA plans as of January 1, 2026
- **Mass General Brigham**: Most primary care providers now out of network for both UHC and BCBS Massachusetts MA plans
- **Lehigh Valley Health Network**: Went out of network with UnitedHealthcare MA on January 25, 2026
- **BayCare Health System**: Went out of network with UnitedHealthcare MA on June 1, 2026
- **NewYork-Presbyterian**: Out of network for most UHC MA members as of June 1, 2026 (current patients protected until August 29, 2026)
- 16+ additional systems nationwide have terminated or restructured MA contracts

**The insurer pullback is simultaneous:** UnitedHealth Group cut approximately **1 million MA members** by dropping plans and exiting 109 counties. Humana anticipates a decline of approximately **425,000 MA members** — a 7.5% reduction. Both UHC and Humana also cut OTC benefits and scaled back supplemental coverage to address financial losses from Medicare Advantage's compressed margins.

**Why this matters for physician practices:** Independent practices affiliated with or referring to these health systems face mid-year disruption to patient volumes, payer mix assumptions, and AR timing. Practices that built revenue models around stable MA contracts face real risk of revenue leakage if they don't actively monitor which payer-network combinations are in flux. A practice submitting claims to the "old" in-network payer for a displaced MA patient will see those claims denied — and often not until they are 60–90 days old.

**Stat:** JAMA February 2026 study: approximately **1 in 10 MA enrollees (2.9 million patients)** were forced to switch plans for 2026 — the largest involuntary disenrollment wave in Medicare Advantage history.

UnitedHealthcare's May 5, 2026 announcement to eliminate prior authorization requirements for **30% of services by year-end 2026** is now moving from announcement to implementation — representing the largest single PA reduction in U.S. insurance history. The changes affect approximately **50 million members** across commercial, Medicare Advantage, and employer-sponsored plans.

**Services being freed from PA requirements (rollout by December 31, 2026):**
- **Outpatient surgeries**: Specified elective and semi-elective procedures
- **Diagnostic imaging**: Echocardiograms — major relief for cardiology practices spending 16+ hours per week on PA administration
- **Outpatient therapies**: Physical therapy, occupational therapy, chiropractic care
- **Pediatric services**: Approximately two-thirds of PA requirements for pediatric procedures eliminated

**How UHC is replacing PA with surveillance:** Instead of blanket prior authorization requirements, UHC is deploying **AI-powered analytics** to detect unusual billing or utilization patterns. Practices should expect that the removal of front-end PA hurdles will be replaced by more sophisticated post-payment audit activity — claims that appear as statistical outliers will receive greater scrutiny than under the PA system.

**Industry PA scorecard update (June 2026):**

- CMS-0062-P Drug PA rule comment period closed June 15; decision expected by Q4 2026 on finalization
- 5 states have enacted significant PA reform legislation effective in 2026 (Massachusetts the most aggressive)

**Stat:** UHC acknowledges that prior authorization is currently required for only **2% of its medical services** — meaning the 30% reduction targets the most administratively burdensome subset of that 2%, the high-friction categories that consume disproportionate staff time relative to their denial rates.

**FY 2027 ICD-10-CM and ICD-10-PCS Updates (Effective October 1, 2026)**

CMS has released the FY 2027 ICD-10 code sets, giving practices a critical 3.5-month preparation window:

**ICD-10-CM (FY 2027): 238 new codes | 4 revised | 21 deleted**
- **Metastatic cancer specificity expanded**: New codes under C78.– and C79.– series add granular anatomical site specificity — oncology and hematology practices must update staging documentation templates to capture site detail these codes require for medical necessity
- **Sex/gender reporting expanded**: New codes provide greater specificity for reporting changes to patient sex characteristics — relevant for endocrinology and primary care practices
- **New platelet disorder codes**: D69.11 (Glanzmann thrombasthenia) and D69.19 (Other qualitative platelet defects) — relevant for hematology billing

**ICD-10-PCS (FY 2027): 101 new codes | 38 deleted | Total: 79,256 codes**
- New device values for **cardiac surgery**: Aortic valve and mitral valve division codes in table 028
- New device values for **spinal fusion**: Custom-made interbody fusion devices in tables 0RG (thoracolumbar) and 0SG (lumbar/lumbosacral) — orthopedic and spine surgery teams need charge master updates before October 1

**GI Denial Alert: Screening-to-Diagnostic Conversion Errors Are the #1 Source of Colonoscopy Denials in 2026**

Gastroenterology practices are experiencing a denial surge driven by a single error pattern: **incorrect handling of screening-to-diagnostic colonoscopy conversions**. When a screening colonoscopy discovers a polyp or lesion requiring intervention, the procedure code must shift — and each major payer applies different rules to this conversion.

**Key error patterns generating denials:**
- Billing **CPT 45378** (diagnostic colonoscopy) alongside **CPT 45385** (polypectomy) on the same date triggers automatic NCCI bundling edits — the therapeutic code absorbs the diagnostic base
- Medicare requires the final code to reflect what was actually performed; failure to convert earns a CO-4 denial
- Procedure notes must explicitly document the screening-to-diagnostic conversion, including cecal intubation status, findings with location specificity, and technique for each intervention — missing documentation results in downcoding

**Stat:** FY 2027 ICD-10-CM adds **238 new diagnosis codes** effective October 1, 2026 — including expanded metastatic cancer specificity codes requiring oncology documentation changes within 3 months.

### Denial Prevention as Competitive Advantage: Closing the 10-Point Gap Between AI-Native Practices and Manual Shops

Revenue cycle performance in mid-2026 is increasingly bifurcated. Practices with AI-native denial prevention are operating at fundamentally different performance levels than those relying on manual claim scrubbing and reactive denial management. The divergence is widening every quarter.

**The 2026 Denial Rate Performance Gap:**

| Performance Category | Denial Rate | Est. Annual Revenue Impact (100-physician group) |
|---|---|---|
| AI-native RCM | ~5.7% | Baseline |
| Best-practice manual | 10–12% | ~$1.2M additional annual loss |
| Average manual shop | 15–18% | ~$2.4M additional annual loss |
| Reactive / no strategy | 20–22% | ~$3.6M additional annual loss |

**What prevention-first practices are doing differently:**
1. **Eligibility verification at every encounter** — not just first visit; the single highest-ROI automation in outpatient RCM
2. **Predictive denial scoring before submission** — ML models flagging claims at >30% denial risk for pre-transmission review
3. **Payer-specific claim scrubbing** — validation against individual LCD/NCD/policy rules, not generic national rules
4. **Denial root-cause tracking by category** — eligibility, coding, authorization, and timely filing denials each require different fixes; mixed tracking misses the pattern
5. **Clean claims rate at 98%+** — practices at 95% leave measurable money uncollected; each point below 98% costs approximately 0.3–0.5 additional days in AR

**Medicare Advantage instability adds new AR pressure in Q3 2026:** Mid-year MA network exits are creating a new AR timing hazard. When patients are unknowingly out-of-network, practices submit claims to the wrong payer — and the resulting denials age into the 60–90 day bucket before the payer-mix error is identified. Revenue cycle teams should implement a weekly MA network status verification against their active patient panels for all payers with open exit negotiations.

**Stat:** **60% of denied claims are never resubmitted or appealed** in average practices — yet 85% of appealed claims are ultimately paid. The recoverable gap averages **$30,000–$80,000 per physician per year**.

## Section 07 — Compliance Corner

### Compliance Alert: Trump Administration Proposes Transparency-in-Coverage Updates; Healthcare Cybersecurity Breaches Hit RCM Vendors

**Price Transparency: Transparency-in-Coverage Rule Update Proposed**

The Trump administration has proposed updates to the **Transparency in Coverage (TiC) rule** — targeting payer Machine-Readable File (MRF) accuracy and usability:
- Insurers would be required to **exclude in-network rate data for services a provider is unlikely to perform** — reducing data noise that currently makes MRFs difficult to use
- **Change logs** must be published when MRF data is updated — giving practices visibility into when payer pricing changes
- **Utilization files** must be separately available — enabling granular analysis of actual claim payment rates versus contracted rates

**For practices entering payer negotiations in the next 12 months:** Once finalized, this TiC update will make contracted rate benchmarking significantly more actionable — change logs and cleaner utilization data will make it easier to identify systematic underpayments before contract renewal.

**Hospital Price Transparency Enforcement Accelerating:**
- CMS issued **10 civil monetary penalties** against hospitals in 2025 — more than doubling the annual enforcement pace of the prior administration
- Enforcement of new expanded requirements (percentile encoding, CEO attestation, Type 2 NPI inclusion) began **April 1, 2026**
- A **35% CMP reduction** is available for hospitals that waive administrative hearing rights — signaling CMS preference for rapid compliance over protracted appeals

**Healthcare Cybersecurity: RCM Vendors Are Now Primary Attack Targets**

Revenue cycle infrastructure has become the **most targeted attack surface in healthcare IT**:
- **TriZetto Provider Solutions** (Cognizant subsidiary): Filed the year's largest healthcare breach report in early 2026, exposing data across more than **2.5 billion annual healthcare transactions** — the highest-volume RCM vendor compromise on record

Simultaneously, UHC's elimination of echocardiogram PA requirements (rolling out through December 2026) provides administrative relief. **Orthopedics:** The FY 2027 ICD-10-PCS tables 0RG and 0SG add new device values for custom-made interbody fusion devices — orthopedic and spine surgery coding teams should begin implementation review now for October 1. Practices should run a pre-surgery MA network status check for all elective cases.

**Oncology:** The FY 2027 ICD-10-CM expanded metastatic cancer codes (C78.– and C79.– series, 3 new codes for anatomical site specificity) require oncology documentation updates by October 1, 2026. **Gastroenterology:** Screening-to-diagnostic colonoscopy conversion errors are now the #1 source of GI claim denials in 2026. NCCI bundling edits automatically deny CPT 45378 + CPT 45385 on the same date; Medicare and MA payer policies on conversion documentation differ materially. GI billing teams should run a 90-day retrospective audit of colonoscopy claims for this error pattern before proceeding with new billing. Denial rates for GI endoscopy procedures in practices without AI-powered claim scrubbing are running above 12% in 2026.

**Radiology:** The proposed Transparency-in-Coverage rule update (requiring insurers to exclude service data for services a provider is unlikely to perform) will directly improve MRF accuracy for radiology services pricing — practices with multiple imaging service lines contracted across multiple payers should monitor the proposed rule's comment period and finalization timeline, as cleaner MRF data will strengthen payer renegotiation positions.

**Mental Health:** Telehealth behavioral health coverage extensions under the CY 2026 PFS remain permanently in effect — providing billing stability for independent mental health practices. The MA network instability in 2026 is less acute for behavioral health (lower MA concentration than procedural specialties), but practices that contracted through exiting health systems may see referral volume disruption. Nationally, telehealth continues to represent 20–35% of behavioral health encounters for most independent practices, making permanent PFS coverage a foundational revenue protection.
