## Section 01 — Regulatory/Payment Updates
### CMS CY 2027 MA Final Rule Takes Effect; Price Transparency Enforcement Active as of April 1

The week of April 21 arrives with two major CMS actions already in motion: the CY 2027 Medicare Advantage & Part D Final Rule published April 6 and the end of the price transparency enforcement grace period on April 1. Both carry direct implications for practices billing Medicare Advantage and hospital outpatient services.

**CMS CY 2027 Medicare Advantage & Part D Final Rule (April 2 / April 6, 2026)**

CMS issued the final rule on April 2 and published it in the Federal Register on April 6, governing Medicare Advantage (Part C) and Prescription Drug Benefit (Part D) programs for Contract Year 2027. The rule takes effect June 1, 2026, with most coverage provisions applying January 1, 2027, and marketing/communications changes effective **October 1, 2026**.

- **MA rate increase:** 2.48%, translating to approximately **$13 billion** in additional payments to MA plans for the 2027 plan year
- **Star Ratings overhaul:** 11 measures focused on administrative processes and provider complaints are removed beginning with the 2027 measurement period
- **Part D structural changes:** Coverage gap ("donut hole") phase eliminated; reduced annual out-of-pocket threshold established; cost sharing in catastrophic phase removed; Manufacturer Discount Program codified
- **Deregulatory agenda:** Reverses several MA marketing and enrollment safeguards introduced in 2023 under Executive Order 14192
- **Provider terminations:** CMS declined to finalize a special enrollment period (SEP) for provider terminations

**Key Stats:** 2.48% rate increase | 11 Star Ratings measures removed | October 1 marketing compliance deadline

**ICD-10-CM April 2026 Update: No New Codes, But 14 Chapter Note Changes Are Live**

No ICD-10-CM codes were added, deleted, or revised — but 14 chapters now contain revised instructional notes effective April 1. Key change: several **Excludes1 notes have been converted to Excludes2**, including under D18 (Hemangioma and lymphangioma) and D49 (Neoplasms of unspecified behavior). Code pairs previously prohibited may now be legitimately billable together.

> **Key Insight:** The removal of 11 Star Ratings measures focused on administrative processes and provider complaints reduces the financial incentive for payers to resolve provider disputes quickly. Expect MA plan dispute resolution timelines to lengthen in the 2027 contract year.

## Section 04 — Coding Updates
### ICD-10-CM Note Changes Now Live in 14 Chapters; Cardiology PCI Overhaul and New AI Diagnostic CT Codes

**ICD-10-CM April 1, 2026: 14 Chapter Note Changes**

No new ICD-10-CM codes, but 14 chapters received revised instructional notes with real billing consequences. Critical change: multiple **Excludes1 notes converted to Excludes2** — code pairs previously prohibited can now be used simultaneously when both conditions coexist. Chapter 2 (Neoplasms) has the most practice-impacting conversions.

**Cardiology: The Most Complex CPT Restructuring of 2026**

- **PCI code deletions:** Codes 92921, 92925, 92929, 92934, 92938, and 92944 are deleted. New code 92930 describes stent placement involving two or more distinct coronary lesions or bifurcation lesions
- **New LER code family:** 46 entirely new codes (37254–37299) for endovascular leg revascularization procedures
- **AI cardiac CT code:** New Category I CPT for AI-based quantification of coronary plaque on cardiac CT angiography — first billable AI diagnostic code in cardiology
- **Remote monitoring updates:** More real-time patient contacts, shorter reporting time spans

**Primary Care CCM/RPM Expansion**

H.R. 1 includes a **2.5% reimbursement increase** for CCM, APCM, and RPM services, with an additional **1.24%** for APM participants. Estimated earnings: **$280–$420 per enrolled chronic care patient per year**.

> **Warning:** Cardiology practices using deleted PCI codes (92921, 92925, 92929, 92934, 92938, 92944) are generating preventable denials. These codes have been invalid since January 1, 2026. Audit all Q1 2026 cardiology claims and resubmit with corrected CPT assignments before timely filing limits expire.

## Section 07 — Compliance Corner
### Compliance Corner: Price Transparency Penalties Now Active; October MA Marketing Deadline Approaches

**Hospital Price Transparency: Enforcement Active as of April 1, 2026**

CMS's 90-day enforcement grace period expired April 1. Penalties are now active.

New requirements (enforced April 1):
- **Median allowed amount** required (replaces estimated allowed amount)
- **10th and 90th percentile allowed amounts** required for disclosure
- **Type 2 NPI inclusion** for all active hospital units (tax codes 27 and 28)
- **EDI 835 ERA files** required as the data basis for calculations

**Active Penalty Schedule**

| Hospital Size | Daily Penalty |
|--------------|--------------|
| ≤30 beds | Up to $300/day |
| 31–550 beds | $10/bed/day (e.g., $3,000/day for 300 beds) |
| >550 beds | $5,500/day |

**October 2026: CY 2027 MA Marketing Compliance Deadline**

Marketing and communications changes from the April 2 final rule take effect **October 1, 2026** — six months away. Practices with MA plan contracts or patient-facing MA materials must begin compliance planning now.

**ICD-10-CM Note Changes: Update Superbills**

14 chapters have new Excludes2 code pair permissions (previously Excludes1). Internal EHR superbills and custom claim edit rules may require manual update even if AAPC Codify and OptumCoding updated automatically.

> **Warning:** Price transparency violations are accumulating daily at up to $5,500/day. If your machine-readable file has not been updated to include median allowed amounts, 10th/90th percentile data, and Type 2 NPIs, financial exposure grows with every day of delay.
